What is Ménière’s disease?

For more information and to see if you may qualify, call 888-205-5801 or click here to see if you may qualify!

Ménière’s disease is a disorder of the inner ear that can affect balance and hearing. People suffering from Ménière’s disease experience a variety of symptoms that may come and go. Some of these are:

  • Dizziness
  • Vertigo (a feeling that your surroundings are moving when there is no actual movement)
  • Nausea
  • Tinnitus (roaring, humming or ringing sounds in your ears)
  • Fullness, pressure and pain in the ears
  • Hearing loss

Episodes of vertigo are considered the most prominent symptom, often lasting at least 20 minutes. People with Ménière’s disease ﬁnd the symptoms they experience may interfere with their activities of daily lives. Currently, there is no cure for Ménière’s disease.
